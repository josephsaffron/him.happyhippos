justice

I was thinking this past weekend, especially in light of recent events, about the issue of fairness. There's a lot of emphasis on “giving people what they deserve”, and there always has been. In fact, the oldest law in history is that of the “eye for an eye”. Even as Christians, we reward those deserving, and we punish those who are not. For example, this past friday night, we went to a resurant, and we recieved pretty bad service. Natural instinct suggests that we leave a low tip. So, then, I did. All night though, I felt guilty, and I kept thinking about it. How could I have let such petty concerns sway my judgement like that? Because of my actions, I may have missed an opportunity to demonstrate the tenets that I believe in. Romans 12 says this: “If your enemy is hungry, feed him; if he is thirsty, give him something to drink. Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good. ” If God gave us what we deserved, it'd all be over right now. But He didn't, and instead, He demonstrated the ultimate act of mercy, forgiving all my wrongdoing. How can I do less, on such a trivial matter?
